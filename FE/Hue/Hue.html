    Hue

    Hue, located in central Vietnam, is a city rich in history and culture. Once the imperial capital of the Nguyen Dynasty, it is renowned for its ancient architecture, including the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Hue Imperial City and the majestic Thien Mu Pagoda. The city is also famous for its peaceful Perfume River, traditional music, and vibrant festivals. Hue's cuisine, featuring dishes like bun bo Hue and banh khoai, offers a delightful taste of Vietnamese culinary heritage. Visitors often come to Hue to immerse themselves in its serene atmosphere and explore the treasures of Vietnam's royal past.

    • Airway system
    • Phu Bai International Airport is the airport serving the city of Hue, Vietnam. The IATA code for Phu Bai Airport is HUI. Phu Bai International Airport is the home base of Vietravel Airlines.
    • Railway system
    • Hue Railway Station is a main station on the North-South railway line. The station is located in Duc Ward, Hue City, Thua Thien Hue Province. Hue Railway Station was built by the French in 1908.
    • Waterway system
    • Thua Thien Hue has many advantages to exploit waterway tourism. The Tam Giang - Cau Hai lagoon system, the rivers, the landscape on both sides of the river and the life of the riverside residents are valuable resources for tourism development.
