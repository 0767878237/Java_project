    Ha Noi

    Hanoi is the capital, a centrally governed city and one of two special urban areas of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam. This is the largest city in Vietnam, located as the political center, one of two important economic, cultural and educational centers in Vietnam. With terrain including the central plain and mountainous areas in the north and west of the city. With an area of 3,359.82 kmÂ², and a population of 8.4 million people, Hanoi is the largest centrally governed city in Vietnam, and is also the second most populous city and has the second highest population density among the 63 provincial administrative units of Vietnam.

    Tourist destination

    • Tomodachi Lang Mit
    • Trai Lang village, Co Dong commune, Son Tay town
      Tomodachi Retreat Lang Mit is like a magical oasis, located 40km from the center of Hanoi. Located in the peaceful green space of the suburbs of Hanoi, this place is inspired by traditional Vietnamese architecture with familiar thatched roof houses, combined with the characteristics of Japanese architecture.
      Reference price: From 1.550.000 VND/night
    • Thang May Village & Resort
    • Chong hamlet, Yen Bai commune, Ba Vi district
      Located in a fresh natural landscape all year round, this place attracts many young people, families and companies to visit and relax on weekends. The Resort creates a beautiful and poetic natural landscape
      Reference price: From 4.500.000 VND/nigh
    • Melia Ba Vi Mountain Retreat
    • Ba Vi National Park, Ba Vi District
      Melia Ba Vi is designed and built based on the architecture of the ancient French period, combined with the surrounding natural landscape. The system of 47 rooms and villas at Melia Ba Vi uses high-class wooden furniture, bringing a very cozy and luxurious feeling.
      Reference price: From 6.582.000 VND/night
    • Airway system
    • Noi Bai International Airport is the largest international airport in Northern Vietnam, serving mainly the capital Hanoi and surrounding areas.
    • Passenger Terminal T1 is the first airport terminal in the country designed by Vietnamese architects, imbued with national identity, like a heavenly gate welcoming visitors to the capital and highly appreciated for its aesthetics.
    • Railway system
    • The urban railway system in Hanoi includes 10 urban railway lines with a total length of about 417.8 km and 3 monorail lines with a length of 44 km, the total length of the network when completed is expected to be 461.8 km.
    • The first two railway lines to be built are line 2A (Cat Linh - Ha Dong section), and line 3 (Nhon - Hanoi Railway Station section).
    • Waterway system
    • With 7 major rivers flowing through it, Hanoi plays an extremely important role in the overall national inland waterway network in general as well as the Northern region and the Red River Delta in particular.
    • Hanoi has 7 major rivers flowing through it with a total length of 556km. Among them are large river systems, widely connected, the Red River section passing through the capital alone is 163km long.
    • Vietnam Military History Museum
      Tay Mo and Dai Mo, Nam Tu Liem district
    • Area 386,600m2. The museum currently preserves more than 150,000 artifacts, including 4 national treasures and many valuable artifacts. Built with a modern, multi-functional design, creating a common space for visitors to interact and experience the struggle for national independence of the heroic Vietnam People's Army.
    • Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum
      2 Hung Vuong Street, Ba Dinh
    • This is a tourist destination in Hanoi that you must visit when you come to the capital. This place preserves the body of the greatest leader of Viet Nam.
    • Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum is open to visitors every morning except Monday and Friday.
    • Ngoc Son Temple, Turtle Towe - Hoan Kiem Lake
      Dinh Tien Hoang, Hang Trong, Hoan Kiem District
    • As one of the symbols of Hanoi. Not only possessing ancient beauty and unique architecture, these works also have great historical and spiritual significance and are a source of pride for the people of the capital.
    • Landmark 72
    • Pham Hung Street, Nam Tu Liem District
      Landmark 72 is a complex of 3 high-rise buildings: hotel - office - apartment - commercial center. This is the 5th largest closed complex in the world. Landmark 72 was the tallest building in Vietnam from 2011 to February 2018 and is the building with the largest floor area in Vietnam from 2011 to present.
    • Temple of Literature - Imperial Academy
    • 8 Quoc Tu Giam Street, Van Mieu Ward, Dong Da District
      This project was built in 1070 under the Ly Dynasty, it is considered the first university of Vietnam, this is also the place that laid the foundation for the country's education throughout the history through many dynasties and now the Temple of Literature - Quoc Tu Giam is still a place to preserve as well as honor history.
    • One Pillar Pagoda
    • Doi Can ward, Ba Dinh district
      The pagoda was built under the reign of King Ly Thai To (1049). Through the ups and downs of history, the pagoda still exists today. It was built in 1955. Currently, One Pillar Pagoda is recognized as the pagoda with the most unique architecture in Asia.
